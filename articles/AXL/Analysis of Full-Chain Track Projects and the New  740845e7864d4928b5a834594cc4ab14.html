2. Analysis of Comparable Full-Chain Projects
2.1 LayerZero

LayerZero (ZRO) is a full-chain interoperability protocol aiming to bridge the gaps between different blockchain networks. Its primary goal is to enable seamless cross-chain communication, allowing assets and data to move freely across chains.

This goal is shared by nearly all full-chain projects, which aim to transfer assets and data across different chains—the core purpose of full-chain technology.

LayerZero achieves this by leveraging the principles of light nodes, designing an ultra-light node mechanism that divides the trust intermediaries into two parts using relayers and oracles, thereby achieving better security at lower costs.

Light nodes operate without interference from external notaries, relying solely on the chain's own security for high decentralization and safety. However, this results in high cross-chain costs, ultimately borne by the user.

To address this, LayerZero's ultra-light node design offers a compromise, trading some security for significantly lower costs. Thus, LayerZero itself does not verify messages; instead, the transaction parties ensure security, and contracts and transaction proofs are verified through oracles and relayers, greatly improving efficiency and speed.

LayerZero raised $135 million in a Series A+ round in 2022 and $120 million in a Series B round in 2023, with its native token ZRO now listed on major exchanges.

2.2 Wormhole

Wormhole is a leading interoperability platform that supports large-scale multi-chain applications and bridging. The platform aims to connect different blockchains, enabling secure and efficient data and asset transfers.

Wormhole originated as a cross-chain bridge between Ethereum and Solana, consisting of on-chain components (emitters, Wormhole core contracts, and transaction logs) and off-chain components (19 guardian nodes and a message transmission network). Benefiting from Solana's prosperity, Wormhole has the highest cross-chain transaction volume.

Wormhole prioritizes cross-chain communication and multi-chain development security and integrity through decentralized validation by Guardians. The Guardians Network, a decentralized network of 19 validator nodes, ensures message integrity and security. Additionally, Wormhole utilizes zero-knowledge proofs to establish new message paths, ensuring security.

In November 2023, Wormhole raised $225 million in a recent funding round, valuing the project at $2.5 billion, attracting investments from Brevan Howard, Coinbase Ventures, Multicoin Capital, Jump Trading, ParaFi, Dialectic, Borderless Capital, and Arrington Capital. Its native token, W, is now listed on major exchanges.

2.3 Particle Network

Particle Network has long been a provider of abstract wallets, with deep expertise in this area. Currently, Particle Network aims to develop a Layer1 chain abstraction module, integrating chain abstraction, wallet abstraction, liquidity abstraction, and gas abstraction, allowing users to manage assets from all chains via a single wallet interface.

Previously operating behind the scenes as a technical provider, Particle Network now seeks to enter the public eye by promoting its abstract wallet solution, which enhances user experience through a more manageable asset interface.

Particle Network's abstract wallet serves as an example of how chain abstraction can simplify user interactions across multiple chains, demonstrating the potential for greater user adoption and satisfaction in the blockchain space.

3.2Cross-chain interoperability

Axelar is a cross-chain interoperability project based on cross-chain technology. On top of the concepts of cross-chain and multi-chain, it proposes the concept of inter-chain, and is committed to providing a unified development environment for all Web3 applications.

To this end, in 2024, Axelar developed the Axelar Virtual Machine (AVM) and launched a number of development tools, allowing Axelar to evolve from a cross-chain layer responsible for message and asset delivery to a cross-chain layer that can program and deploy smart contracts to perform more complex operations.

To some extent, Axelar's inter-chain concept is an upgrade of the cross-chain and multi-chain concepts. Its core is to take advantage of the Axelar network and use the Axelar network as a central interaction node in a multi-chain network. By developing and deploying applications in this central node and then expanding to other networks, it can greatly improve efficiency and reduce costs, and for users, it will also have a smoother user experience.

In terms of cross-chain solutions, Axelar chose a "trinity" cross-chain layer with gateways as points, validators as lines, and Axelar network as surfaces as a cross-chain solution. Among them:

The gateway is mainly responsible for communication and cross-chain execution functions. On the source chain initiated by the cross-chain, the gateway is responsible for initiating the corresponding request, and on the target chain received by the cross-chain, the gateway is responsible for receiving and executing the corresponding message to complete the cross-chain operation.

On the EVM chain, the gateway exists in the form of a smart contract, while on Cosmos and other non-EVM chains, the gateway exists in the form of a Dapp. The multi-signature key that controls the gateway is jointly held by all validators. The share of the shared key held by each validator is determined by the number of AXL tokens staked by it. The key will only take effect when the share of the key submitted by the validator who confirms the message exceeds the threshold.

The cross-chain messages sent by users on each chain will first interact with the gateway. After receiving the message, the gateway will generate an event in the background, and then the relay will pick up these events and submit them to the Axelar network for processing.

The validator is mainly responsible for message verification and network consensus. When a cross-chain event is submitted to the Axelar network, the validator node will start working, querying its RPC port on the source chain node to check whether the submitted event can be observed, and then confirming the existence of the event and voting to approve the event as legal. The validator will then package these events into blocks and confirm these blocks through the proof-of-stake consensus.

The Axelar network is mainly responsible for processing all cross-chain requests and paying the corresponding Gas fees. In this process, Axelar needs to use the tokens on the target chain to pay the Gas fee.

In order to facilitate the use of users, so that they do not need to prepare tokens on the target chain and Axelar to pay the Gas fee, Axelar created and deployed a smart contract called Gas Receiver. The Gas Receiver contract estimates the total gas fee required on the source chain, Axelar network and target chain, charges the user the native token on the source chain as the gas fee at one time, and then converts these native tokens into AXL, target chain tokens and other required tokens to complete the gas fee payment on behalf of the user. In this process, the Gas Receiver generally charges more tokens than the actual payment amount, and returns the excess gas fee to the user's account after the transaction is completed.

Such an operation can make the user experience very smooth, greatly coordinate the gas of the entire chain, and make unified use of it. If it can be achieved, it will be a huge breakthrough.

3.3Security and Scalability

Axelar's security is mainly guaranteed by multi-signature keys and the network consensus layer. In short, Axelar needs to reach a certain number of nodes to confirm the authenticity at the same time when transmitting cross-chain information and performing cross-chain operations in order to be judged as valid, and the gateway can play a role.

In addition, when all cross-chain events are packaged, they need to be confirmed by the Delegated Proof of Stake (DPoS) consensus mechanism before a new block can be generated.

At the same time, in order to reduce the risk of voting rights being concentrated in a few stakeholders due to different AXL staked shares, Axelar also introduced the "square voting" mechanism at the consensus level, that is, when a validator votes, it needs to stake one unit of AXL tokens to obtain one share of voting rights, but if it needs to obtain two shares of voting rights, it needs to stake two squares, that is, four units of AXL tokens, and so on. The token shares owned by the validator node need to be squared before the corresponding number of voting rights can be obtained.

After limiting the validator nodes with high stakes, the network's decentralization has been further improved.

The existence of the above two mechanisms ensures that Axelar has better security than common cross-chain bridges.

In terms of scalability, Axelar, as a public chain, can execute smart contracts, which is already very important.

This means that Axelar can integrate with other public chains and Dapps more conveniently, quickly and at low cost, and even act as their "cross-chain layer" in the concept of modular blockchain to help these projects complete cross-chain operations, while also effectively improving the user experience.

For example, an API released by Axelar can help the integrated Dapp generate a one-time deposit address, These addresses can receive cross-chain funds of any token from any wallet on any integrated chain, allowing users to get an interactive experience no less than that of centralized exchanges during the cross-chain process.

Secondly, by expanding the General Message Passing (GMP) function, Axelar can not only realize cross-chain assets, but also support complex cross-chain function calls and cross-chain state synchronization, which greatly improves the scalability of Dapps that cooperate with Axelar.

3.4On-chain data and financing

According to Axelar's block browser, as of 23:00 on July 18, 2024, Axelar generated a total of 2,171,081 transactions, of which 1.59M were GMP (General Message Passing) transactions, accounting for about 73.23%.

GMP transactions are mainly used to call smart contracts on other chains connected by Axelar on one chain. The difference between them and ordinary cross-chain transactions is that the designated object is a smart contract, while ordinary cross-chain transactions are only responsible for transferring an asset from the source chain account to the target chain account. Therefore, after 2023, the rapid increase in the number of GMP transactions reflects the continuous improvement and activeness of Axelar's cross-chain ecology.
